As we shift left with agentic practices, we leave behind the right. As code
becomes less and less of the thing that is delivered, we'll leave behind code
centric platforms and practices. Forget traditional CI pipelines. Forget
change/pull requests. Forget build failures. We define what we want and the code
delivery pipeline can "poof" it into existence.

<!--more-->

The world of software development is moving so fast right now. I, for one, have
been struggling to try and see around the bend. The bends keep coming and
they're so sharp. However, I think I have a sprout of an idea of one possible
future. I've never used the term "shift left" before but I've heard that a lot
recently. This post if about what we leave behind on the right when we shift
left.

## Shift left?

When I talk about shifting left, think about a timeline style diagram of the
software development lifecycle. On the left you have raw product ideas. the
lifecycle moves through different phases and tasks rightward to the end product
all the way on the right. Software developers have been pretty far right on this
chart touching the actual product through code. As we shift left we are moving
away from the last mile delivery parts and more toward higher level thinking.

## 

As we move left on that chart, we have to leave some things on the right behind.
Way on the right just before the product is the delivery. Traditionally, we've
had many humans toiling on changes to a codebase carefully crafting them and
planning their integration into the mainline independently. It was the coming
together of those changes that we built a lot of ceremony around. We have
changesets and pull requests. We have CI pipelines. All of these in service to
report back to the human how their work is fitting into the greater codebase.
